TREE APP

Tree App

Case Study

Tree App will help you to create a feeling of compassion interconnectedness and stewardship towards trees. It will redefine your position towards them through reflecting on our past, present and future.

Objective

The app aims to help users collaboratively collect, discover and spread their stories about trees in their surroundings. Through uploading fotos and text they will locate personal tree stories on a map


Goals

To create an app that helps to put trees more in focus and mind of people. It will create a connection between people and trees and lets users learn about personal human-tree stories. To be used outdoors easily, it should be intuitive and easy to understand.


Challenges

The challenge is to create an app that will redefine our position towards trees and through reflecting on our past, present and future with them. The aim is to create a more contemporary and fun experience.

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed two location based recommendation apps, Raaago and Foursquare as well as different tree mapping apps and websites. From these I conducted an UX and SWOT analysis.

Interviews

To understand potential users more in depth, I interviewed three persons who all have different approach to trees, but all have an interest in botanics. It was important for me to interview people with different expertise and profession to see their own needs and goals.

Informed by the interviews, I created user personas highlighting the key points, interests and needs of potential users.



The interviewers mentioned that they would like to have an app that informs them not only about the type of trees, but also about the stories behind and surrounding them. They would like to explore the relationship between humans and trees in a playful way. Ready to be used outdoors, the interface should be intuitive and simple.


Out of my findings and after defining the MVPS, I created six different user stories combined in three different user flows about uploading content, interacting with other users and having some playful tasks.

#1


As a person who likes to share my experiences about trees, I want to write and post them so that others can read it too.

#2


As a person who likes to take photos, I want to share mine with others, so that trees are not anonymous anymore.

#3


As a person who wants to challenge my thinking, I want to have some task, so that I can see things from a different point of view.

Using the Crazy 8's method, I imagined low fidelity wireframes according to the previous developed user flows. After choosing the best suited ones, I turned them into an early prototype to test the usability on potential users.

I conducted some live early prototyping usability test with Marvel App with different users and changed the userflow according to the painpoints of the testing and designed mid-fidelity wireframes.
